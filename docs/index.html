PIE-V

Framework for Constructing and Benchmarking Mistake Aware Egocentric Procedural Videos

Olga Loginova and Frank Keller

University of Trento · University of Edinburgh

Paper Code Release

About

PIE-V is a pipeline to create examples of erroneous procedure execution where mistakes are not random, scripted, or staged, but human-plausible deviations with explicit procedural consequences and, when appropriate, recoveries. It constructs controlled mistake-aware egocentric procedural videos from clean keystep-annotated procedures.

The pipeline combines phase- and load-aware error planning, semantic role edits, recovery sampling, LLM rewriting and judging, and targeted video generation for affected windows.

From Clean Keysteps To Mistake-Aware Videos

We show how the PIE-V pipeline and evaluation protocol work on procedural videos from Ego-Exo4D. Source videos and original Ego-Exo4D annotations remain local inputs governed by the Ego-Exo4D access terms.

Ego-Exo4D Subset

PIE-V starts from 17 Ego-Exo4D keystep tasks, covering COVID test procedures, bicycle repair, CPR, and cooking tasks such as salads, noodles, omelets, tea, pasta, coffee latte, and sushi rolls. The subset contains 50 takes: three videos per task, except Cooking Sushi Rolls, where only one take is available, and COVID Test, where a fourth take is added because it is the most frequent task.

Structured Error Planning

The planner samples 102 mistakes across the 50 takes. Sampling is conditioned on video duration, step complexity, task complexity, procedure phase, working-memory load, and semantic step information from semantic role labeling. The semantic resources and generated text examples document the intermediate outputs.

Recovery Mechanism

PIE-V adds 27 sampled corrections that are sampled conditionally on detectability, action type, latency, and repair strategy, including stopping to fix an action, rolling back and redoing a step, undoing an extra step, or performing a missed step later.

Human Evaluation

Five annotators evaluated the resulting scenarios with a nine-part rubric designed for plausible erroneous procedures and grounded in human perception of mistakes. The aggregate annotation summary and annotation template document the rating fields.

Error Taxonomy + Correction

PIE-V maps generated mistakes and existing datasets into shared procedural categories.

WE

Wrong Execution

Wrong object, side, amount, location, orientation, or manner.

D

Deletion

A required step is skipped, leaving a missing prerequisite.

S

Substitution

A plausible incorrect action replaces the intended step.

I

Insertion

An extra unnecessary step enters the procedure.

T

Transposition

Two locally plausible steps are executed in the wrong order.

+
C

Correction

A recovery step detects and repairs a specific mistake.

Human Rubric

The rubric separates local plausibility, procedural coherence, and video grounding.

Error Validity

What we measure: whether the proposed deviation has procedural impact.

Why it matters: filters out harmless execution variation before downstream scoring.

Human/Text Plausibility

What we measure: how likely a real person is to make this mistake in context.

Why it matters: keeps generated mistakes aligned with human behavior.

Confusability

What we measure: how difficult the mistake is to notice during execution.

Why it matters: captures detectability and subtlety.

Procedure Logic

What we measure: whether the full procedure becomes logically broken, with annotator confidence.

Why it matters: connects local edits to global procedure feasibility.

Sequence Consistency

What we measure: whether the edited step order remains executable.

Why it matters: detects ordering failures after edits and transpositions.

State-Change Coherence

What we measure: whether object states and prerequisites remain possible across the trace.

Why it matters: catches impossible or missing state transitions.

Taxonomy Fit

What we measure: which WE, D, S, I, T, or C label best explains the marked deviation.

Why it matters: makes heterogeneous mistake datasets comparable.

Video Plausibility

What we measure: how natural the generated or annotated visual behavior looks.

Why it matters: separates procedural quality from visual artifacts.

Text-Video Grounding

What we measure: whether the full textual trace matches the video at episode level.

Why it matters: catches mismatches that accumulate after a mistake or recovery.
